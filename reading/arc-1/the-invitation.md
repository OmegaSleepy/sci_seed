A cool breeze blew in from the opened window, but Mark did not even bother to close it. His eyes were glued only to the screen glowing in front of him. With his pasty complexion and heavy, dark eye shadows, he seemed more like a ghost lurking around in his room rather than an eighteen-year-old boy winning yet another national informatics Olympiad. Socializing was tiring for him, while math and logic were simply logical. It was only within himself that he was able to work with variables, identify patterns, and construct worlds using codes.

In a daze of scrolling through the social media feed he had been doing for the past half hour, he felt himself beginning to blink heavily. It was already very late. He logged into his computer, as per routine, and went into his mail box.

However, what met his eyes was a fresh mail with a sender’s ID stating: *National Space Agency of Bulgaria.

Mark froze. He rubbed his eyes as he looked at the screen in front of him. His first reaction was to believe that someone had breached his system and was attempting to steal money from his bank account. However, upon closer inspection of the email, he found out it was legitimate.

[[arc-1/documents/nsab_recruitment_offer|NSAB_RECRUITMENT_OFFER.eml]]

"I've been invited…" whispered Mark into the dark, empty room, his voice rough from the lack of speech for so many hours. “…by the National Space Agency of Bulgaria to become… no, wait. I’m not becoming an astronaut. So what the hell is a ‘seed’?”

He spun around in his gaming chair, glancing at the bedroom door. "Should I tell Mom and Dad?" He checked the bottom corner of his monitor. It was past 3:00 AM. "Nah, they're asleep. My brother is out cold, too." He turned back to the screen, his heart drumming against his ribs. "I can't go to bed. I... is this even real?"

Determined to find an explanation for the strange email, he opened up a new browser window and called up his favorite AI chatbot assistant.

`> Query: Provide the definition of the term "seed" within the scope of aviation and space flight.`

After watching the little loading dots flash for a moment, the text appeared on the screen:

`> Response: The term "seed" does not pertain to aviation and space flight. It usually relates to botany, programming languages, or encryption initialization.`

Frustrated, Mark sighed heavily and rubbed his tired eyes. "Alright, I give up," he mumbled to himself and shut down his computer monitor, immersing the room in darkness. "I'm going to bed now. Who knew that I would ever fall for something like that."

Mark did not share this email with anyone. He even tried to forget about it since it looked like too good to be true.

As weeks turned into months and summer approached, high school became deserted as most students stopped showing up. However, Mark remained resolute and never missed classes; there was something inside him that urged him to be punctual no matter how meaningless those classes seemed to him.

It was on one of his last days in high school when Mark heard a familiar voice while leaving the school premises.

“Hi, Mark!” shouted Michaela with contagious enthusiasm as she ran up to him. “You should really take a look at this! I just got an email from the National Sp-"

"Michaela, that’s an obvious scam," Mark cut her off, his voice flat.

They had known each other since their first year of high school. Michaela was a happy-go-lucky teen from another class. She was the complete polar opposite of Mark when it came to academics - while Mark loved programming and maths, she loved the complex science behind biology and chemistry.

After hearing out Michaela, Mark felt more confident that the email was a genuine mass spam. "I actually saw once that spoofing email signatures is not that hard, this makes sense." Mark rested his chin on his hand and continued thinking. "The contents of the email also feel very malicious... wait they did not ask me do download anything or fill in any details..."

Confused by Mark's sudden loss of thought, Michaela reached for her phone. "So the email says that I can check at the local police department if I want to enroll. I wanted to ask you if you wanted to come with me, since you know - you live nearby".

Mark not sure of anything anymore accepts and they go together. While going to the station Mark read the email multiple times, but still was not sure of the legitimacy, it felt like that in a few minutes someone was going to jump them and tell them they got pranked. But this did not happen and Michaela went in the police station. Mark waited outside for minutes, more nervous with each passing minute. "There is no way, it has to be real. But what if Michaela is in with the fraudsters and when I enter they will surprise me... there is no chance the police are with them too." Frustrated Mark enters in the building and goes to the Inquiry desk and shows the clerk the email.

A quick look at the screen made the desk clerk freeze and hurriedly tell Mark to wait. Soon enough, a policeman dressed in a uniform appeared at the desk and gestured for Mark to follow him. With palms sweaty from nervousness, Mark followed the man through a gloomy cement corridor where worst-case scenarios raced through his mind. At the end of the passage, Mark approached a thick door through which he could hear a faint but unmistakable voice – it belonged to Michaela.

Opening the door for Mark, the policeman revealed an interrogation room where two men were standing while Michaela was seated on a stool in front of a huge computer screen. One of the men was wearing a white lab coat and round spectacles, while the second one looked like an army man dressed in camouflage clothes.

"And you are?" the man in the white lab coat asked, turning his attention to Mark.

Standing in front of the men, Mark’s pale face contrasted sharply with dark circles under his eyes. He definitely did not wish to be there, but under the circumstances, Mark was experiencing some strange defense reaction that made his usual stammering go away – now his speech became unnaturally clear and formal.

"Mark Ivanov, sir. I was invited to a specialized program by the National Space Agency of Bulgaria via email, sir."

The scientist nodded briefly and indicated a spare chair. "I was just finishing my explanation to the girl… why me?" the man muttered to himself, obviously annoyed at having to go through the entire process all over again. "Alright. I will do the abbreviated version. We are delegates from the NSAB. We have been sending thousands of very specific emails to the best high school and college students in the country. The UN released the World Cataclysm Report twenty-four years ago, in 2126. This year, 2150, we will begin carrying out the directive of the report."

The scientist stopped and cleared his throat before reaching for the mug on his desk. "It details how crucial it is to select one thousand youth to be put into cryonic sleep for fifty thousand years. Your job will be to come back and re-colonize the Earth."

Mark raised his hand like he was in a university lecture. The scientist nodded at him.

"I have questions, sir, if I may," Mark said, his voice clipped. "What exactly do you mean by 'recolonize'? Why fifty thousand years? And what specific variables did you use to select us?"

Placing the mug gently back down onto the table, the scientist replied, “Everything is explained in the report. Earth is bound to lose its ability to support any kind of life at all by 2200 AD, provided that all human industrial activities are not ceased by 2175 AD. This fifty-thousand-year period of time is what the biosphere needs to cleanse the toxicity of us from itself. As for our criteria? We need the best youth available – intelligent, versatile, and very able individuals, who are able to rebuild human civilization from scratch. The rest of mankind would be sent into deep space via generation ships, looking for a new planet to live on. Upon waking up, your task is going to be contacting the deep space colony.”

Speechless by the magnitude of the news, Mark leaned back in his seat and sighed quietly. The mask had slipped away. “You’re asking me and Michaela? And my family? They… they won’t be there, correct?”

The military man gave a gruff sigh, putting his arms across his chest. "Andonov, they are asking the same questions. This is pointless, why couldn't these two just come together?" His gaze was intense as he turned to look at Mark. "No, boy. Your family goes back. Because of the rules involving genetic diversity for a pool of only one thousand, we can't bring your family along. That's too many genetic ties for us to deal with statistically."

Mark slowly nodded his head. The cold reasoning made sense.

As the soldier was talking, Dr. Andonov was flipping through pages of his digital notebook. He froze for an instant, showing an expression of surprise on his face which quickly smoothed itself out again. "Well. Seems like you two are more than usual candidates. Michaela, there is one thing I didn't mention, because I've been told that I can't reveal it without the pair candidate leaders being present. But since Mark is here..."

This information affected them in different ways. Mark got an unpleasant feeling in his stomach, and in school he used to be a background character in any group assignments, never participating in any discussions and leadership activities. Michaela, on the other hand, straightened up in her chair and understood why she had been spending the last hour in this conversation. For she was a natural leader.

"Please do not jump to conclusions yet," Andonov advised her. "There are other candidates that are equally capable of fulfilling this position. In case you are not able to cope, somebody else will take over the mission." Andonov leaned towards Michaela, lowering his voice. "In case you decide to join, you will spend your next five years learning. You will become familiar with some advanced science which you never knew existed before and learn classified state secrets and also get trained to lead the one thousand people from the Bulgarian team."

"The Bulgarian team?" Michaela asked, raising an eyebrow. "What does it mean? Does it mean there are other teams?"

"There are twenty nations altogether, who will be planting a seed group of one thousand," Andonov explained honestly, tapping his desk. "Just to be honest, more than twenty nations were selected but some of them are not expected to reach quotas, especially when compared to others. Each group will consist of one thousand people and you two are amongst the best candidates to lead our capsule."

He made some notes on his tablet and lifted his eyes from them, looking sternly. "But there is one last matter that you have to take into account before making your decision. Prior to entering the stasis, you, candidate leaders, have to go through a mandatory medical procedure. Its death rate is rather high. The statistics were distorted in early trials owing to improper tests, but now we boast the success rate of about sixty-five percent."

"Okay, I accept the conditions," Michaela answered without hesitation. She glanced at Mark, the thought had struck her like lightning - signing that paper and parting from him meant they could never meet again, but the future was so heavy to give it away for purely personal considerations.

"And what about you, young man?" asked Dr. Andonov gently, putting a bottle of water in front of Mark. "You look a little pale. Let me tell you that in case the procedure turns out to be fatal, your family will be paid a grant of several million Euros. In case of its success... Well, the biological effect of the serum is truly remarkable, Mark Ivanov. You can live forever."

Mark stared at the scientist. Initially, it seemed like Mark had to sell his soul to the devil wearing a white coat. But there was one particular word that resonated inside him – the word *forever*. “I can see the stars! Earth, fifty thousand years later… ” The mathematician part of Mark turned on the risk/reward calculus machine and tried to balance the slow death of an approaching end with eternity of the Seedling project.

His eye twitched. Mark glanced at Michaela, then at his shaking hands and ultimately straightened his gaze at the door.

“Ad Astra!” Mark announced firmly. “Dr. Andonov, I agree!”

Without uttering a single word, the scientist pushed two hefty papers across the table. All the legalese was written with a goal to waive all the fundamental humans rights, but none of them read the terms and conditions anymore. Inside that room, under the harsh fluorescent light, two teens were looking at the dawn of a new world.
