After accepting the once in a lifetime opportunity, Mark and Michaela went outside. They walked for a bit, not talking to each other, but thinking - each for completely different things. 

"What I am going to tell mom and dad?". After the adrenaline rush of the deal ended, Mark's reasoning kicked in. "They can't do anything, but... they are going to be so mad and I can't hide that..."

Michaela on the other hand was checking her phone and actively was planning out her final goodbyes on her phone. "Mom and dad will be so proud of me!" she happily said out loud.

Mark heard that and he felt alone again. "Can you, give me tips on how to tell them. You are good at this..."

Michaela quickly connected the dots and she put down her phone. "Wanna me to do the honers?"

"No, what?" Mark quickly raised his hands in denial, but he gave the idea a second chance "But, they know you and they trust you. To be honest, if you go with me, they will probably assume that we are coming out at first."

"Uh, don't be dummy. It will be fine, don't worry - I will handle it for you!". As she said that, Michaela did a cringy pose that made Mark giggle. "Hey don't laugh! This girl will secure your future you know?"

"Fine, fine, Ms. Speaker. Don't pose like that in front of my parents thou-". Mark coughed, the air in the city was bad. In recent years, cost cutting and city planning was getting really bad in their town. The park they were going through to reach Mark's apartment was one of the worst. Even with high tech automatic cleaning robots, the lack of actual foliage was a big problem in the dry summer day. To give credit to the city, they did attempt to plant new ones, but the soil was too acidic for any sapling to actually survive, most of the park was just genetically modified grass. 

After some time and chatting, both teens were in front of Mark's apartment. Michaela has been there many times and her parents are friends with Mark's, but they always come as guests together, not just Michaela. 

"Remember, act natural" Mark told Michaela as he was reaching for his keys.

"To be honest, I need to give this advice to you man, they are going to take it well." Michaela said with confidence and reassurance with her voice as she was reaching to take off her shoes. "Just leave me to handle the talking, right?" 

Mark nodded and opened the front door. "You can put your shoes outside if you want" As he opened the door he saw his mother's figure. In Mark's eyes every time he had to talk to his parents felt like he was put into interrogation. His parents really cared for him, but he became used to solve his own problems and talking to others to solve a problem felt like he had to slow down and waste valuable time to him.

"Hello Mark, hello Michaela." His mother's voice echoed in his chest, he felt like this could be the end of his happy life with his family. He knew what he signed less than a hour ago - he won't see his family and they won't see him. For him, once he enrolls to the program, he will be dead for his parents.

"Hello mom". A small tear began collecting on his left eye. He was overthinking, but without a safety switch he spiraled out and went deeper and deeper with each passing moment.

"Hello Ms. Ivanova! We have some news to share." Trying to ignore Mark's tears, Michaela went inside and continued to speak. "So, is Mr. Ivanov here, it is somewhat important". She pointed to the bedroom to Mark in the hopes he can stabilize a bit.

"Yes he is here, what is the matter. Don't tell me, Mark I always knew you had an eye for her!" Mark's mother began sweating. Michaela was the only girl in Mark's life and Mark's and Michaela's parents expected for a long time that they will end up together, but they wanted for things to play out naturally, so they did not interfere. "Honey! Come to the living room, Mark and Michaela have news! Leave your stuff in Mark's room and come to the living room, be hasty!"

Mark and Michaela leave their bags in Mark's room. His brother who often acts as Mark doesn't exist did note of Michaela's appearance, but he did not greet her. While changing, Mark thought "Why am I acting like that, they are not going to be mad. Ha-ha-ha, I am 18 and they have no authority over me, even if they disagree - I won't be stopped from going." Spiraling manically, Mark hit his toe in his bed frame. "Oh yea, the scientists did mention that if we fail we go back home, other people are competing for our position too. If I fail to become a leader I need a place to bounce back... I need my parents damn it." 

Frustrated, Mark walks to the living room. His mother and dad already chatting with Michaela quickly switch their topic when Mark comes in the room.

"So what is so important. I get it is not you dating, but I don't get what could it be otherwise..." Mark's father is an intelligent man, he was the reason Mark even began learning programming.

"Me and Mark got invited - ". Mark didn't sit, he looked different, but his mind was still scattered.  "Oh Mark don't stand stood, come sit next to me!"

"I want to share the news myself, if that is not a problem"

"But I thought I was going to, fine." Michaela disappointed that she lost the opportunity crossed her arms and looked tense.  

"Mom dad, as Michaela started sharing, we got invited to a special mission by the National Space Agency and we signed a contract an hour ago."

"What did you-" Both of his parents jumped of their chairs, their worry was mixed with anger, but all was rooted in their want for their son to be safe.

"That's why I should've told the news, Mark." Michaela spoke up and interrupted Marks' parents "It is an offer that we couldn't resist. They offered us to lead a group of young scientists to safe Earth. But the problem is that we might never see you or anyone we know ever again"

"Why, why, why is that? Why couldn't you just work as a programmer or an engineer? Mark what is wrong with you!" Mark's father looked tense, his tone was sharp and anger slipped though his mouth. He was extremely forgiving when it came to normal life stuff, but when it came to work or science he became infinitely demanding and expected everything to happen to the dot.

"Dad, you know me. You taught me to be what I am. Don't you think I didn't think about it, I will be the last programmer that Bulgaria will have ever!" Tears began dripping from his eyes. "This is the opportunity to cement me and our family as the cornerstone of civilization!" 

"Don't cry, nobody has died yet." His father let a sigh and then a grunt, he was disappointed, but he thought he lacked context. "A family story from long ago - you great-great-great-grandfather was born by the turn of the 21st century. They invented AI when he was around your age and he was probably one of the best programmers that the world could've ever wanted. When you tell me you are going to be the last programmer that Bulgaria will ever have I understand just one thing - you are plan on ruining your family's legacy. How can you assure me that this *program* will keep our legacy safe?"
